# Progression

## Principles

- Increase one training factor at a time: intensity, volume, or density.
- Progress slowly (5–10% per week) and include an easy week every 3–4 weeks.
- Prioritize quality over quantity: keep interval power high and consistent.
- Use recovery weeks and test weeks to monitor adaptation.

---

## Sample 6-week progression (5-minute VO2 intervals)

- Week 1: 5×5' @ FTP+3–5% with 5' easy recovery (total high-intensity time 25')
- Week 2: 6×5' @ FTP+3–5% with 5' recovery (30')
- Week 3: 6×5' @ FTP+5–7% with 4' recovery (30')
- Week 4 (deload): 4×5' @ FTP+2–3% with 6' recovery (20')
- Week 5: 7×5' @ FTP+5–7% with 4' recovery (35')
- Week 6 (peak/test): 5×5' @ FTP+8–10% with 5' recovery or perform an FTP/VO2max test

Adjustments:
- If fatigue accumulates, repeat a week or insert an extra deload.
- If adaptation is rapid, increase interval count or slightly raise target power.

---

## Monitoring

- Track RPE, heart rate response, and power consistency across intervals.
- Look for improved interval power, faster recovery heart rate, or lower RPE at the same power.

---

## Example session templates

- Short session: 4×5' @ FTP+4% / 5' easy
- Standard session: 6×5' @ FTP+5% / 4–5' easy
- High-volume session: 8×5' @ FTP+5% / 3–4' easy (only for advanced athletes)
